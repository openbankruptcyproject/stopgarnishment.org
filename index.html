Stop Wage Garnishment

How to End Garnishment and Protect Your Income

How Wage Garnishment Works

After a creditor obtains a court judgment, they can request a garnishment order. Your employer is legally required to withhold a portion of your paycheck and send it to the creditor. Federal law limits garnishment to 25% of disposable earnings or the amount by which your weekly pay exceeds 30 times the federal minimum wage, whichever is less.

Income That Cannot Be Garnished

Federal law protects: Social Security, SSI/SSDI, veterans benefits, federal retirement pensions, railroad retirement, and most public assistance. Once these funds are in your bank account, they may need to be claimed as exempt if levied. Are you judgment proof?

How to Stop Garnishment

File a claim of exemption: If your income is exempt or the garnishment causes undue hardship, file with the court.

Negotiate: Contact the creditor and propose a payment plan in exchange for releasing the garnishment.

File bankruptcy: The automatic stay stops all garnishment immediately -- often the fastest option. The automatic stay explained.

Pay the judgment: Once satisfied, the garnishment ends.

Bankruptcy: The Immediate Solution

Filing bankruptcy triggers the automatic stay, which immediately stops wage garnishment. Your employer receives a notice and must stop withholding. In some cases, you may even recover garnished wages from the 90 days before filing. Chapter 7 eliminates the underlying debt in 4-6 months. What happens in bankruptcy.

Special Rules for Specific Debts

Child support: Up to 50-65% of disposable income. Cannot be stopped by bankruptcy.

Student loans: Up to 15% through administrative garnishment (no judgment needed).

Taxes: IRS can garnish without a court order. Varies by situation.

Credit cards/medical: Requires judgment first. Limited to 25% of disposable earnings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How fast does bankruptcy stop garnishment?

Immediately upon filing. Your employer typically receives notice within 1-3 days.

Can I get back wages that were already garnished?

Possibly. Wages garnished within 90 days before filing may be recoverable as a preference payment.

Can my employer fire me for a garnishment?

Federal law prohibits termination for a single garnishment. However, multiple garnishments may not be protected in all states.

Can bankruptcy stop wage garnishment?

Yes. Filing bankruptcy triggers the automatic stay under 11 U.S.C. Section 362, which immediately halts all wage garnishment. Your employer receives a notice to stop withholding, typically within one to three business days of filing. Both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 provide this protection.

How much of my wages can be garnished?

Federal law limits creditor garnishment to 25% of your disposable earnings or the amount by which your weekly pay exceeds 30 times the federal minimum wage, whichever is less. Child support can take up to 50-65%. Some states set lower limits than federal law.

How quickly does bankruptcy stop garnishment?

The automatic stay takes effect the instant your bankruptcy petition is filed with the court. Your attorney or the court clerk notifies your employer, usually within one to three days. If garnishment continues after notice, the creditor may face sanctions under Section 362(k).
