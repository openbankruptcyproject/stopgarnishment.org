Federal Garnishment Limits

Federal law limits how much creditors can garnish from your wages -- usually 25% of disposable earnings

← Home

The 25% Rule

Under the Consumer Credit Protection Act (CCPA), creditors can garnish the lesser of 25% of your disposable earnings or the amount by which your weekly disposable earnings exceed 30 times the federal minimum wage. "Disposable earnings" means your pay after mandatory deductions like taxes and Social Security -- not your gross pay.

At the current federal minimum wage of $7.25 per hour, 30 times that amount is $217.50 per week. If your weekly disposable earnings are $217.50 or less, your wages cannot be garnished at all. If your disposable earnings are between $217.50 and $290.00, only the amount above $217.50 can be taken. Above $290.00, the 25% limit applies.

Different Rules for Different Debts

Not all debts follow the 25% rule. Child support and alimony can take up to 50% of disposable earnings (60% if you are not supporting another spouse or child, and an additional 5% if payments are more than 12 weeks overdue). Federal student loans can take up to 15% through administrative garnishment. Federal tax levies have their own formula based on filing status and dependents, and can sometimes take more than 25%.

State taxes follow state rules, which vary widely. Some states mirror the federal limits, while others allow higher or lower percentages.

State Limits May Be Lower

Some states set garnishment limits lower than the federal maximum. For example, some states cap garnishment at 10-15% of disposable earnings or protect a larger portion of wages. When state and federal limits conflict, the law that results in less garnishment applies.

This means you should always check both your state law and federal law to determine the actual maximum that can be taken from your paycheck. The more protective limit wins.

Multiple Garnishments

If you have multiple creditors seeking garnishment, total garnishment is generally capped at 25% (for non-support debts). A second creditor must wait until the first garnishment is satisfied before their order takes effect. However, support obligations take priority and are calculated separately, which can mean total withholding exceeds 25%.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can creditors garnish from my wages?

The lesser of 25% of disposable earnings or the amount exceeding 30 times the federal minimum wage per week. Child support can take up to 50-65%.

Can I be garnished below minimum wage?

If your disposable earnings are at or below 30 times the federal minimum wage ($217.50/week), your wages cannot be garnished by private creditors at all.

Do state limits override federal limits?

When state limits are more protective (lower percentage), the state limit applies. The law that results in less garnishment always controls.

Can I have multiple garnishments at once?

Total garnishment for non-support debts is generally capped at 25%. Support obligations are calculated separately and take priority.
